Paddings, Margins and Borders:

Padding: The spacing inside an element-box or content - this is spacing between the element and the box containing the element, padding helps us shift content from the edges of its container. Padding can be allocated with units such as px, rem or em, the larger the value of the unit, the larger the spacing.

Margins: The spacing outside an element-box or content - this is the spacing between and element and other boxes around it. Margin heps us shift an element away from touching other elements outside it. Margins can be allocated with units such as px, rem or em, the larger the value of the unit, the larger the spacing.

Border: The border is used to wrap the content and padding (if padding is allocated). Borders are like lines that can be styled as dots, solid lines or dashes, to whatever color and thickness - using px, rem or em. This section is wraped by a brown border.
